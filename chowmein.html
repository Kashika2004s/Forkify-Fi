Chowmein

Chowmein is a popular Chinese dish that has become widely enjoyed
and adapted in various parts of the world, including India. It consists
of stir-fried noodles, typically made from wheat flour, mixed with a
variety of vegetables, meats, and sauces.

Chowmein is a versatile and flavorful dish that can be served as a main course
or as a side dish. It is often enjoyed hot and fresh, garnished with
chopped green onions or cilantro for added freshness and flavor.

Ingredients:


    Ingredients for Chowmein

  • 200 grams chowmein noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 bell pepper (capsicum)
  • 1 cup cabbage, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1/2 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon chili sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped green onions for garnish

How to Make Chowmein



To prepare Chowmein:

  1. Cook the chowmein noodles according to the package instructions until

  2. Drain and rinse the noodles under cold water to stop the cooking process. Set aside.

  3. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.

  4. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger, and saut√© for about 30 seconds until fragrant.

  5. Add the sliced onion, julienned carrot, sliced bell pepper, cabbage, broccoli
    florets, and sliced mushrooms (if using) to the skillet.

  6. Stir-fry the vegetables for 3-4 minutes until they are slightly tender but still crisp.

  7. Add the cooked chowmein noodles to the skillet along with soy sauce, oyster
    sauce, and chili sauce.

  8. Toss everything together until the noodles are well coated with the sauce and
    the vegetables are evenly distributed.

  9. Season the chowmein with salt and pepper to taste, and continue to stir-fry
    for another 2-3 minutes until everything is heated through.

  10. Remove the chowmein from the heat and transfer it to a serving dish.

  11. Garnish with chopped green onions if desired.

  12. Serve the vegetable chowmein hot as a delicious and satisfying meal or side dish.
    Enjoy your homemade chowmein!
