Spring roll

Spring rolls are a popular dish in various Asian cuisines,
known for their crispy exterior and flavorful fillings. They are often served
as appetizers or snacks and can be found in Chinese, Thai, Vietnamese, and
other Asian culinary traditions. The fillings can vary widely depending on regional
preferences and personal tastes, but they typically include a combination
of vegetables, meat, or seafood.


Overall, spring rolls are a versatile and delicious dish that can be enjoyed
as a snack, appetizer, or even a main course, and they offer a delightful
combination of textures and flavors.

Ingredients:


    Ingredients for Spring roll:

  • 8-10 spring roll wrappers
  • 2 cups shredded cabbage
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1 cup thinly sliced bell peppers
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 2-3 spring onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying
  • 1 teaspoon chili sauce
  • Chopped spring onions for garnish

How to Make Spring Roll



To prepare Spring roll:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat. Add ginger and garlic, saut√©
    for a minute until fragrant.

  2. Add shredded cabbage, carrots, bell peppers, and bean sprouts to the pan.

  3. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are slightly softened but still
    crisp.Season the vegetables with soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and pepper.

  4. Mix well and cook for another minute. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool down.

  5. Take one spring roll wrapper and place it on a clean surface in a diamond
    shape, with one corner pointing towards you.

  6. Place a spoonful of the vegetable filling in the center of the wrapper.
    Fold the bottom corner over the filling, tuck it under the filling, and roll
    tightly halfway.

  7. Fold the left and right corners towards the center.Brush the top corner with
    water and roll up tightly to seal the spring roll.

  8. Repeat with the remaining wrappers and filling.Heat oil in a deep frying
    pan or wok over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the spring rolls
    in batches and fry until they are golden brown and crispy.

  9. Remove the spring rolls from the oil and drain them on paper towels to remove
    excess oil.

  10. For the dipping sauce, combine soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, honey
    or sugar (if using), and chili sauce (if using) in a small bowl. Mix well.

  11. Serve the crispy spring rolls hot with the dipping sauce on the side.
